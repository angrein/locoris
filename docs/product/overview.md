# Locoris Product Overview

Locoris is a local-first workspace for structured knowledge, visual thinking, planning, and private sync.

The app is not positioned as only a notes editor. Its product strength is the combination of:

- rich text notes;
- canvas work;
- orbital map navigation;
- planner, calendar, habits, goals, and review;
- AI tools integrated into editor and canvas workflows;
- local vaults;
- readable backups and exports;
- encrypted sync through hosted cloud, self-hosted sync, or Google Drive.

## Core Surfaces

### Vaults

A vault is a local workspace with its own projects, folders, tags, notes, canvases, assets, planner data, settings, and sync bindings.

Locoris can keep multiple local vaults on one device. A local vault can be regular or private.

### Notes

Notes are rich text documents built around BlockNote. They can belong to projects and folders, carry tags, link to planner items, and be exported in readable formats.

### Canvas

Canvas documents are visual workspaces based on Excalidraw. They are first-class content entries inside the same vault hierarchy as notes.

### Orbital Map

The orbital map shows projects, folders, notes, canvases, tags, and contextual signals as a navigable knowledge space. It is a product pillar, not a decorative graph.

### Planner

Planner is a first-class Locoris surface for tasks, calendar, recurring tasks, habits, goals, review, reminders, and temporal map signals.

Tasks are commitments. Habits are rhythms and check-ins. Calendar aggregates scheduled work and habit signals without turning habits into ordinary tasks.

### AI

AI integration is currently Gemini-focused. AI actions are native to the editor and canvas rather than a detached chat surface.

The user deliberately invokes AI actions. Large or risky text changes should prefer preview/diff style UX unless a user setting says otherwise.

## Supported Platforms

Locoris must be considered across:

- web desktop;
- macOS desktop through Tauri;
- Windows desktop through Tauri;
- Android through Tauri;
- mobile portrait web;
- Android portrait;
- tablet landscape and portrait.

Mobile portrait should receive its own UX for complex flows, especially sync, settings, pickers, and sheets.

## Commercial Direction

The local app should remain useful without an account.

The commercial product direction is:

- free local-first application;
- paid Locoris Cloud for hosted encrypted sync, storage, web access, history, recovery, and support;
- self-hosted sync and Google Drive sync kept as trust-building alternatives;
- later paid additions such as teams, publish, hosted AI, and advanced cloud backups.
